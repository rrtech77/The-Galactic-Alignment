Apocalypse or the Beatific Vision of Apocatastasis?

⚡ Apocalypse

Revelation — usually mediated by supernatural beings. Discloses transcendental powers, eschatological scenarios, and judgement of the dead. Linear, decisive, and final in Christian interpretation: advancing progressively toward an irreversible conclusion.

✦ Apocatastasis

Restoration — first in Aristotle (4th cent. BC); cosmological, astrological, cyclical. The return of all things to their natural state. In Gnostic thought, Sophia becomes re-integrated into the Pleroma. Equivalent to resurrection: the restoration of Man with God and the recovery of purity.

Astral apocatastasis in Hellenistic astrology refers to the periodic return of the stars to an initial position — the duration of a Great Year. Heraclitus set the Great Year at 10,800 years; the Stoics refined it as the sidereal Great Year scheduling cycles of destruction (ekpyrosis) by flood or fire, followed by regeneration (palingenesis).

Plato spoke of eight planetary spheres in the Timaeus without using the word apocatastasis. Cicero defined it as a restoration of the seven planets to their points of departure, or a return of the stars to their initial positions. Proclus attributes apocatastasis to the Assyrians (meaning Chaldeans), but Hellenistic astrology also drew from Egypt. In the 1st cent. BC, Figulus conceived a palingetic cycle as being a great cosmic week crowned by the reign of Apollo. Vergil said, "A great order is born out of the fullness of the ages … now your Apollo reigns." The return of Apollo corresponds to the Golden Age. In the Mithraic mysteries the last door is made of gold (the Sun), since the order of planetary doors in reverse represents a backward progression through time.

"In Plato's day, the idea of a Great Year was known, but it was not connected with the possibility that the stars shift. In fact, an inviolable dogma of the day proclaimed that the stars were fixed." — John Major Jenkins

The Greeks interpreted earlier Babylonian insights (which hinted at the precession of the stars) in a highly questionable way: the flood that ended one Great Year was believed to be timed by all the planets aligning in one sign, Cancer, and then the conflagration to end the next Great Year was thought to occur when a similar alignment of planets occurred in the opposite sign, Capricorn.

The female Aion Achamoth (also known sometimes as Pistis-Sophia) awaits the return of the saviour (her consort or syzygy) who will be restored to her [in astrology, syzygy also refers to opposite points at which a planet conjuncts or opposes the Sun, or to eclipse points].

In Christianity the coming of the saviour was more a historical process which unfolded with linear rather than cyclical time, advancing progressively and definitively rather than being regressive. In the gospels Elijah would restore all things (Mark 9:10). In Malachi 3:23, Yahveh will restore hearts and lead them back — salvation of creation becomes reconciled with God, returning to Eden. Marcus said universal restoration coincides with a return to unity. For Basilides the beginning was Non-Being, which meant the restoration should return to nothingness.

Apocatastasis is equivalent to resurrection — the restoration of Man with God, the recovered purity of heart and piety towards God. The devil becomes redeemed and evil annihilated. The Catholic Church says the means of regeneration provided for this life do not remain available after death, so that those dying unregenerate are eternally excluded from the supernatural happiness of the beatific vision or Apocatastasis. These souls enjoy and will eternally enjoy a state of perfect natural happiness; and this is what Catholics usually mean when they speak of the limbus infantium, the "children's limbo."

The Precession of the Equinoxes

Our orientation to the larger universe is constantly shifting. Something causes the intersection of the celestial and ecliptic planes to cycle around the ecliptic over an estimated period of 26,000 years, while the tilt of the Earth relative to the Sun's axis keeps seasons in sync.

The accepted theory — lunisolar precession — holds that combined gravitational effects of the Sun and Moon on the Earth's equatorial bulge cause the polar axis to wobble clockwise in a slow circle, like a spinning top. The alternative view favoured here is that our Sun orbits in a binary system with a companion star (possibly an invisible brown dwarf) over a 26,000-year period. In this model the whole solar system's orientation to the fixed stars rotates, with no gyroscopic wobble required.

Precession alters the positions of stars, constellations and the Galactic Plane such that the Sun's position on equinoxes or solstices periodically aligns with the Milky Way. This precession of the solstices was believed to be responsible for the succession of World Ages — the precessional cycle also called the Great Year, Platonic Year, or Plato's Year.

Key Distinction The solar system's sinusoidal oscillation through the galactic midplane — which takes roughly 500,000 years per half-cycle — is entirely distinct from precession and has no bearing on the 2012 Galactic Alignment. Conflating the two is a common and significant error in popular accounts.

The Two Gates — Moon and Sun

In Pythagorean theology — the major source of western mysticism — the gate of the Moon lies in Cancer (or Gemini), through which souls descend into incarnation; the gate of the Sun lies in Capricorn (or Sagittarius), through which souls ascend to heaven. The Moon represents the mind — the Manas of Purusha, the Vohumano (Good Mind) of Zoroastrianism, the Keeper of the Seed — whose vigour is conveyed through the Chinvat bridge (the periphery of the earth's vortex) to impregnate matter. The Sun gate is the place of dissolution, fire, reabsorption.

"At this point we shall discuss the order of the steps by which the soul descends from the sky to the infernal regions of this life. The Milky Way girdles the zodiac, its great circle meeting it obliquely so that it crosses it at the two tropical signs Capricorn and Cancer… Cancer, the portal of men, because through it descent is made to the infernal regions; Capricorn, the portal of gods, because through it souls return to their rightful abode… the soul, descending from the place where the zodiac and the Milky Way intersect, is protracted in its downward course from a sphere… into a cone." — Macrobius, Commentary on the Dream of Scipio, Ch. XII (5th cent. AD)

The image of the soul as a cone of light refracted through a crystal ball into matter derives from Egypt and was preserved by Neoplatonists like Macrobius and Numenius. The doctrine of two doorways in the cosmic cave traces to neo-Pythagorean philosophers Numenius and Cronius (2nd cent. AD), and earlier still — the idea of solstices as soul-gateways is already alluded to in Homer's Odyssey.

By the time of Macrobius (ca. 410 AD), the gates had migrated to Sagittarius (Golden Gate — Gods descend, souls ascend) and Gemini (Silver Gate — souls descend to earth), reflecting the galactic crossing points. Adrian Gilbert records this tradition from the ancient Greeks: the Silver Gate of Gemini lies above Orion at the centre of the Duat — the Egyptian Netherworld mapped on the Giza plateau; the Golden Gate of Sagittarius lies where the ecliptic crosses the Milky Way, near the Galactic Centre itself.

Version 1 — Solar Calendar

The Mayan Haab consisted of 18 months of 20 days plus a short month of 5 days at the winter solstice: 18 × 20 + 5 = 365 days. Every 20th year, 5 extra days were added to the short month: 365 × 20 + 5 = 7,305 days ÷ 20 = 365.25 days (equivalent to the leap year concept). On the 400th year only 2 extra days were added instead of 5, yielding: 19 × 7,305 + 7,302 = 146,097 ÷ 400 = 365.2425 days — identical to the Gregorian calendar.

Version 2 — Lunar Cycle

A perfect repeatable lunar cycle of 235 moons in 19 years — already demonstrated in Mayan glyphs at Copan — is equivalent to the Metonic cycle, independently discovered by Babylonians and Greeks. According to the Mayan ratio of 149 moons equalling 4,400 days: 4,400 ÷ 149 = 29.5302 days per lunation. Multiplied by 235 moons = 6,939.597 days ÷ 19 years = 365.2420 days — more accurate than the Gregorian calendar.

Calendar Interlocking It takes 73 rotations of the Tzolk'in (260 days) and 52 rotations of the Haab (365 days) to repeat the same date (73 × 260 = 52 × 365 = 18,980 days). The calendaric wheel also divides 52 Haab years into 4 parts of 13 years each — connecting to the 4 directions of the cosmos.

Mayans do not consider themselves polytheists. Their supreme deity is Ahau — analogous to Jehovah or Allah as creator of everything. There were thirteen gods of the thirteen heavens and nine gods of the nine underworlds. Between the upper heavens and the underworlds of the night and death was the earthly plane — depicted in art as a two-headed turtle lying in a great lake.

The Mayan Great Chain of Being as described in the Popol Vuh includes: Ahau (Heart of Heaven and Heart of the Earth, creator of all); three Lightning Gods forming a trinity (together making up the Heart of Heaven); nine Creator-Formers who fashioned the first humans from nine drinks of maize gruel (two earlier human-like races — of mud, then of wood — had been destroyed); and the Four Cardinal Directions.

Tepeu and Gukumatz (also known as Kukulcan, or the Aztec Quetzalcoatl) were two of the first beings to exist — as wise as sages — and are called the Creators and the Forefathers. Huracan (Heart of Heaven) acted as a storm god doing the actual creating while Tepeu and Gucumatz guided the process. Mayan divinities are not discrete but merge into each other.

The Myth of Hun-Hunahpu

According to the Popol Vuh, Hun-Hunahpu ("One-Hunahpu," a calendrical name) is the father of the Maya Hero Twins — Hunahpu and Ixbalanque — by a virgin. Paired with his brother Vucub-Hunahpu, Hun-Hunahpu was defeated in the ballgame by the lords of the Underworld (Xibalba) and sacrificed; his head was suspended in a trophy-tree where it changed to a calabash whose spittle impregnated Xquic, a daughter of a Xibalba lord. She fled the underworld and conceived the Twins. After the Twins defeated the lords of Xibalba, they recovered the remains of their father and uncle but could not resuscitate them.

It has been asserted that the Classic Period Maya believed Hun-Hunahpu to have been reborn as maize. The scene of the Tonsured Maize God rising from a turtle carapace (the "tomb" of the earth) is interpreted as Hun-Hunahpu resurrected, flanked by the Hero Twins as the maize deity's sons. A male maize deity (God E) is present in all three extant Maya books; the Classic period distinguished two male forms: a Foliated and a Tonsured Maize God.

Scholarly Caveat The identification of Hun-Hunahpu with the Maize Deity remains a theory in need of corroboration. The Popol Vuh itself does not unequivocally state Hun-Hunahpu to be, or become, identical with the maize, nor is he resurrected by his sons. The hieroglyphical name of the Tonsured Maize God — possibly "Hun-Nal-Ye" — is not recognisable as that of Hun-Hunahpu. Even assuming the Twins are "resurrecting" the main maize god, there is no compelling reason to identify him as Hun-Hunahpu.

Pacal's Sarcophagus and the Sacred Tree

Jenkins interprets the famous sarcophagus lid of Palenque ruler Pacal as showing the Sacred Tree as the doorway to the underworld — the crossing point of the Milky Way and ecliptic. The Milky Way tree serves as an extension of Pacal's umbilicus, his entrance into life and into death. Lord (Ahau) Pacal, equated with the sun by divine kingship, is portrayed "entering" the Sacred Tree.

"The day when the sun conjuncts the crossing point of ecliptic and Milky Way — when the Milky Way arches overhead from the region of Polaris (Heart of Sky) — is the only date when the Sun/Lord could jump from the ecliptic track and travel the Milky Way up and around the vault of heaven to the region of Polaris, there to enter the 'Heart of Sky.' This date changes with precession." — John Major Jenkins

The dark bifurcation in the Milky Way — caused by interstellar dust clouds — appears as a dark road beginning near the ecliptic and stretching along the Milky Way toward Polaris. The Quiché Maya call it xibalba be (the "road to Xibalba"); the Chorti call it the "camino de Santiago"; the ancient Maya called it the "Black Road" in the Popol Vuh. The Hero Twins Hunahpu and Xbalanque must journey down this road to battle the Lords of Xibalba.

The Mayan World Tree — symbolic of the creation and ordering of the world, the axis of Earth and Sky with roots in Xibalba and branches reaching the heavens — is called Wakah Chan ("raised up sky") in the Temple of the Cross at Palenque. Atop it sits Itzam-Yeh, the "principal bird deity," possibly representing the zenith or the pivot of the heavens about the North Star. On Pacal's sarcophagus lid, the dead king is depicted falling along the World Tree into Xibalba; an accompanying text reports that he has "taken the road" — the Sak be, or white road, a name for the Milky Way.

The First Moment — Quirigua Stela C

The creation story on Quirigua Stela C gives the most detailed account of the first moment. The text describes the first event as the "appearance or manifestation of an image" (halhi k'ohba) — specifically, three stone-settings (u tz'apwa tun):

1A jaguar throne stone placed by the Paddler Gods at Na-Ho-Chan ("House Five-Sky")
2A snake throne stone set up by an unknown god at Kab-Kah ("Earth-town")
3A water / ocean throne stone set up by Itzamna

The Long Count calendar start date is inscribed on the side of Stela C: 13.0.0.0.0 / 4 Ahaw 8 Kumk'u (August 11, 3114 BC). The start date is carved as 13.0.0.0.0 rather than 0.0.0.0.0 because it also represents the ending of the previous 13-baktun cycle.

On the Pot of the Seven Gods, God L sits on a jaguar throne facing six others. The text narrates that "on 4 Ahaw 8 Kumk'u it was set in order, Black-is-its-Center" (Ek'-u-Tan) — the pre-creation universe was black because the sky had not yet been lifted from the Primordial Sea. A second creation pot shows Na-Ho-Chan-Witz-Xaman ("Female-Five-Sky-Mountain-North") with snake-headed cords — the Classic-period version of the kuxan sum, the "living cord" or sky umbilicus famous in Yucatan legends.

The Three Hearth Stones — Orion

At Quirigua, Palenque, and other sites, the scribes specified the stones were set at "Lying-down Sky First Three-Stone-Place" (Ch'a-Chan-Yax-Ox-Tunal). Schele identified this First-Three-Stone-Place as Alnitak, Saiph, and Rigel — the three stars of the lower triangle of Orion. The K'iche' call Orion Je Chi Q'aq' ("dispersed fire"); its three hearth stars are Oxib' Xk'ub' ("three hearthstones"); and Nebula M42 is Q'aq' ("fire"). The turtle of creation — which cracks open for the rebirth of the Maize God — is also located in the sky as Gemini or Orion, identified in the Bonampak murals. The three stars on the turtle's back matched the belt of Orion.

Another inscription records: "on 4 Ahaw 8 Kumk'u, was seen the first image of the turtle, the great divine lord" (Chan Ahaw Waxak Kumk'u ilahi yax k'oh Ak Chak Ch'u Ahaw). The turtle — associated with Gemini/Orion — was the double-headed turtle that cracks open for the rebirth of the Maize God (Hun-Nal-E, "One-Maize-Kernel").

The Raising of the World Tree

The Temple of the Cross text records that 542 days after 4 Ahaw 8 Kumk'u, Hun-Ye-Nal (GI the father) "entered the sky" (och ta chan). The Tablet of the Cross describes this sky-raising: "On 13 Ik' end of Mol, it was made proper, the Six (Raised-up) Sky, the Eight-House-Partitions" — establishing the Wakah-Chan (the Milky Way arching south to north) with eight partitions corresponding to the four world directions and the corners between them.

Schele proposed that the Classic-period terms for north were na or nal ("house") and xaman, while Villela suggested that nohol ("south") is noh hol — "great hole." This is cosmologically apt: the crown of the World Tree is to the north; its trunk and roots (the great hole) are to the south.

Schele also determined that the double-headed serpent bar wrapped around the branches of the World Tree represents the ecliptic. All beings hanging from these serpent frames — emerging from their mouths or sitting inside their arches — represent objects travelling along the ecliptic: planets, the Sun, the Moon.
